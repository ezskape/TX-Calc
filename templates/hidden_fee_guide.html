Your Texas Electricity Hidden Fee Guide

How to avoid overpaying — even when the advertised rate looks cheap.

Why advertised rates are misleading in Texas

Many plans promote an eye-catching cents-per-kWh number that only applies at one usage tier. The real cost often includes additional delivery charges, base fees, and credits that shift the effective rate once your usage changes.

The difference between energy charges and TDU delivery charges

Your bill is made up of two separate parts: the energy charge from your retail provider and the delivery charge from your local Transmission and Distribution Utility (TDU). TDUs apply both a per-kWh delivery rate and a fixed monthly delivery charge that every customer pays.

Common hidden fees

  • Base charges: fixed monthly fees that raise your true rate at lower usage.
  • Minimum usage fees: penalties when you do not hit a required kWh threshold.
  • Bill credits with thresholds: discounts that disappear if you miss a usage band.
  • Time-of-use fine print: free nights or weekends paired with higher daytime rates.

How to read the Electricity Facts Label (EFL)

Look for the "Average Price" table, then read the sections on fees and credits. The EFL shows pricing at 500, 1,000, and 2,000 kWh, plus any minimum usage fees, base charges, and TDU delivery charges. If the EFL calls out a bill credit, confirm the exact usage range required to earn it.

A quick checklist for any plan

  1. Confirm the energy rate and TDU delivery rate separately.
  2. Add the fixed base charges to your expected monthly total.
  3. Check whether you qualify for bill credits at your typical usage.
  4. Compare your effective rate at your real kWh usage—not just the advertised number.
